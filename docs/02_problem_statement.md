# Problem Statement

## Overview

Modern job searching is highly fragmented and administratively demanding.

Professionals typically manage job descriptions, CVs, recruiter conversations, interview preparation, portfolio updates, networking activities and career planning across multiple disconnected tools.

As a result, career decisions are often reactive rather than strategic — and significant time is lost to repetitive evaluation, tracking, and document preparation.

Candidates frequently invest substantial effort tailoring applications and re-analysing similar roles without understanding which opportunities provide the greatest return on effort or how their overall career strategy is progressing.

---

## Current Challenges

A modern job search involves many independent activities:

- Searching for suitable roles
- Evaluating job descriptions
- Tailoring CVs
- Writing cover letters
- Responding to application questions
- Contacting recruiters
- Preparing for interviews
- Updating portfolios
- Tracking applications
- Monitoring professional visibility
- Planning future skill development

These activities are rarely connected through a single decision-making process. Much of the work is repetitive: reading job descriptions, extracting requirements, comparing roles against experience, updating trackers, and preparing similar materials for each application.

---

## Existing Tools

Current career tools generally solve only isolated problems.

Examples include:

- CV builders
- Cover letter generators
- Job boards
- LinkedIn
- Applicant tracking spreadsheets
- AI writing assistants

While each tool provides value, they do not provide holistic career intelligence.

They generate content but rarely assist with strategic decision-making or reduce the cumulative administrative burden of running a search.

---

## Core Problem

The challenge is not only generating documents.

The challenge is making consistently good career decisions **and** avoiding unnecessary repetition in the work required to act on those decisions.

Professionals need guidance on questions such as:

- Which jobs are worth applying for?
- How much effort should be invested?
- Which portfolio project should be highlighted?
- Which technical gaps genuinely matter?
- What should be prioritised today?
- Is professional visibility improving?
- Which activities produce the greatest career return?

These questions require reasoning rather than document generation alone. Answering them repeatedly for every new role consumes time that could be spent on high-value activities such as interview preparation, networking, and skill development.

---

## Opportunity

Recent advances in Large Language Models create the opportunity to build intelligent career decision-support systems that also reduce repetitive administrative work.

Rather than replacing existing tools, an AI Career Copilot can orchestrate them through a unified reasoning process — automating structured analysis and tracking where safe, while keeping important decisions and externally visible actions under human review.

The result is a system that continuously evaluates opportunities, prioritises actions, explains recommendations and adapts to changing career goals.

---

## Product Vision

The AI Career Copilot aims to transform career management from a reactive collection of tasks into an intelligent, evidence-based decision-support workflow that helps users spend less time managing their search and more time advancing their careers.

The immediate objective is to help the repository owner secure a suitable AI Engineering role sooner by improving opportunity selection and reducing job-search effort.

The long-term objective is to improve career outcomes through better decisions and well-scoped automation — not through unchecked automation of applications or externally visible communications.
