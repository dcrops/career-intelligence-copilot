# Problem Statement

## Overview

Modern job searching is highly fragmented.

Professionals typically manage job descriptions, CVs, recruiter conversations, interview preparation, portfolio updates, networking activities and career planning across multiple disconnected tools.

As a result, career decisions are often reactive rather than strategic.

Candidates frequently invest significant time tailoring applications without understanding which opportunities provide the greatest return on effort or how their overall career strategy is progressing.

---

## Current Challenges

A modern job search involves many independent activities:

- Searching for suitable roles
- Evaluating job descriptions
- Tailoring CVs
- Writing cover letters
- Responding to application questions
- Contacting recruiters
- Preparing for interviews
- Updating portfolios
- Tracking applications
- Monitoring professional visibility
- Planning future skill development

These activities are rarely connected through a single decision-making process.

---

## Existing Tools

Current career tools generally solve only isolated problems.

Examples include:

- CV builders
- Cover letter generators
- Job boards
- LinkedIn
- Applicant tracking spreadsheets
- AI writing assistants

While each tool provides value, they do not provide holistic career intelligence.

They generate content but rarely assist with strategic decision-making.

---

## Core Problem

The challenge is not generating documents.

The challenge is making consistently good career decisions.

Professionals need guidance on questions such as:

- Which jobs are worth applying for?
- How much effort should be invested?
- Which portfolio project should be highlighted?
- Which technical gaps genuinely matter?
- What should be prioritised today?
- Is professional visibility improving?
- Which activities produce the greatest career return?

These questions require reasoning rather than document generation.

---

## Opportunity

Recent advances in Large Language Models create the opportunity to build intelligent career decision-support systems.

Rather than replacing existing tools, an AI Career Copilot can orchestrate them through a unified reasoning process.

The result is a system that continuously evaluates opportunities, prioritises actions, explains recommendations and adapts to changing career goals.

---

## Product Vision

The AI Career Copilot aims to transform career management from a reactive collection of tasks into an intelligent, evidence-based decision-support workflow.

The objective is not to automate job applications.

The objective is to improve career outcomes.
